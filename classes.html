Ellen's Advice on UW Classes

I started my UW-Madison journey undecided on my major, but I chose to start down the pre-business path freshman year. At the end of freshman year, I applied to the Wisconsin School of Business, and I got in as a Marketing major. During my sophomore year, I tacked on a Digital Studies certificate. On this page, I'd like to share a quick rating for the all the classes I've taken during my time at UW-Madison.

Five-Star Classes

  • COM ARTS 200: Introduction to Digital Communication
  • COM ARTS 155: Introduction to Digital Media Production
  • LIS 351: Introduction to Digital Information
  • MKT 355: Marketing in a Digital Age
  • MKT 365: Services Marketing (abroad)
  • SOC 134: American Racial and Ethnic Minorities
  • COM ARTS 605: Digital Studies Capstone

Four-Star Classes

  • INTL BUS 320: Intercultural Communication in Business
  • GEN BUS 307: Business Analytics II
  • JOURN 201: Introduction to Mass Communication
  • ECON 101: Principles of Microeconomics
  • PSYCH 202: Introduction to Psychology
  • POLI SCI 140: Introduction to International Relations
  • MHR 365: Organizational Behavior (abroad)
  • GEN BUS 301: Business Law

Three-Star Classes

  • MKT 445: Digital Marketing Analytics
  • GEN BUS 306: Business Analytics I
  • PHILOS 243: Ethics, Organizations, and Society (abroad)
  • MKT 310: Marketing Research
  • ENVIR ST 113: Environmental Studies - Humanistic Perspective
  • GEN BUS 120: Personal and Professional Foundation in Business
  • ACCT I S 211: Introduction to Managerial Accounting

Two-Star Classes

  • MKT 305: Consumer Behavior
  • ART 107: Introduction to Digital Communication
  • MKT 300: Marketing Management
  • MKT 460: Marketing Strategy
  • GEN BUS 300: Professional Communication
  • CNSR SCI 111: Finance Life Skills
  • ACCT I S 100: Introduction to Financial Accounting

One-Star Classes

  • MHR 300: Managing Organizations
  • OTM 300: Project and Process Management (abroad)
  • COM ARTS 260: Communication and Human Behavior
  • ECON 102: Principles of Macroeconomics
  • FINANCE 300: Introduction to Finance
